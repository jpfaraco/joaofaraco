In September 2012, Rio de Janeiro hosted Insert 2012. This was the first edition of a project that started two years ago by seven designers who wanted something new for design, art and culture in Rio. An idea that came out of informal chats, emails, Skypes, and evolved into a project that touched the lives of everyone involved, and mobilized the national design scene.

"What matters is to make it happen" had always been our motto, and that "an idea on paper is worthless." So after many meetings, congresses, conferences and seeing the opportunity for something like this, the project had to leave the paper - a large design conferencein Brazil. In Rio!
An event to call our own, for Brazil.

The goal of Insert is to give the participant a glance over the multidiciplinarities of design applied to the real world, and after having experienced it, inspire their own work and attitudes in their daily activities.

For this, the event featured eight national and international talks, two round tables, six mini-cases from students and small agencies, three special case studies, 5 workshops and a visitors area with a design fair, exhibitions and activities, and to top it off, a super party at the end of the first day.

All this washed by the warmth of the culture and the environment in Rio, at the Centro Cultural Ação da Cidadania, an amazing warehouse at the heart of the city's port zone.

 
 

Talks

The main attractions were the one-hour talks, especially the international ones, whose speakers came from countries like USA (Joshua Davis and Aaron Duffy), Argentina (Pablo Alfieri), United Kingdom (Thomas Manss) and Germany (Eike König).

Brazilian speakers also showed their inspiring work, and how the various aspects of national design are strengthening and solidifying the design culture in Brazil. Among them, Claudio Peralta, effects supervisor at Conspiração Filmes, André Stolarski, creative director at Tecnopop, and Ricardo Bezerra and Maíra Rahme, from Tátil Design.

Round tables

Also on the main stage and for the first time, two meetings discussed two opposing but related subjects.

The round table "Design Today" was a discussion about the ways of the national design today, the realities of the current market in professional and academic aspects, presented by Fernando Reule, Rafo Castro, Renato Facchini and Bruno Porto, under the mediation of Prof. Luciano Tardin.

The round table "Design Forever" was a journey through the history of design in Brazil, recalling the values ​​that were built to get where we are today, and learning from the legacy that was left. The table was enlightened by Nair de Paula Soares, Rafael Rodrigues, Roberto Verschleisser and Irene Peixoto, under the mediation of Dr. Livia Rezende.

Case studies

Three special case studies were also under the spotlight on the main stage: Fabio Lopez, presenting his social criticism projects that use design as a communication tool; Henrique Nardi and Mariana Ochs, showed how a proper use of typography can strengthen the message of a publication; Priscilla Midori and Victor Marcello, presenting the Nosotros Project, a trip through Latin America in search of the relationship of each culture with the local creative works.

Mini-cases

In addition to the lectures and round tables, the main stage also received 6 mini-cases of newly graduated students and small agencies, who showed their work and presented their cases. The mini-cases were selected from proposals submitted by dozens of students and young professionals, and those who took the stage were Pedro Segreto and Marcelo Alt, from Chaos! studio; Elenay Oliveira, from Leaf magazine; Renato Carvalho, from Startaê, Carlos de Oliveira Junior and Marlus Araujo, presenting their crowd-funded Bicicletorama project; Rodrigo Minguez and Pedro Perdigão, from EPA!; And Bee Grandinetti, presenting her graduation project "Sem Tarja Preta".

Workshops

For those who wanted to get their hands dirty and learn the practical way, Insert hosted 5 workshops. The workshops were sessions short enough for the participant not miss the rest of the event, but complete enough for a good profit and inspiring exploration of a particular subject.

The classes were at most 20 people, allowing close contact between the participant and the teacher, and covered a wide variation of subjects. The workshops were: Focus Thanks to Reduction, administered by Eike König; Projection Mapping with Augusto Amaral; Creating Innovative Business Plans with Ronaldo Porto and Cassiano Farani; Sketchbook Binding, with the master Renato Alarcão, and Lomography: Introduction and Practice, with Philippe Machado, of Lomography.

Besides the closed workshops, Senai offered free creative workshops throughout the day at their booth.

Design fair

The first half of the warehouse had attractions even for those who didn't want to attend the talks.

Upstairs, Project "Feirinha" was a special space for designers who are launching their brands could sell their products for the right people. Of the dozens of projects received, 12 were selected, and set up their booths on the second floor, selling products ranging from bicycles, wooden sunglasses, and temporary tattoos.

Besides the Feirinha, the area has also served as a space for partners to assemble their product sales and brand activations. Among them, Wacom, Senai, Compactor, Infnet, RioBooks and Meninos.

Exhibitions

Downstairs was where the exhibitions happened – the Printgram photo wall, which brought together more than 1,400 photos taken by the participants; Project Nosotros expo; Camiseteria, which exposed the winning t-shirt designs selected from the online contest; Expo 60 Degrees, in partnership with La Estampa, was an unconventional but very carioca graffiti installation, where artists would customize beach umbrellas on the spot.

Activities and Party

And for no one to stand around in such a big space, during the day, LEV electric bikes and a Red Bull skate park, and on Saturday night, PUSH! party.

PUSH! is a party from Belo Horizonte, which is now in its 14th edition in Minas Gerais, but this was the first edition away from home, bringing over 900 people to the dance floor at Insert. Featuring the very latest in the electronic scene and adjacent styles, as well as the whole aura of urban culture, PUSH! brought renowned DJs such as Sexistalk, Vitor Sobrinho, the two-time champion Nedu Lopes, Xeréu and Nepal. That's not counting the MCs from SHUP! and the Pimpão bear, who pumped the audience and distributed their bright accessories, ensuring a sweaty night until 05:00 AM.
 
 

Public

Insert's public was a reflection of the content we brought – an event for students who seek extra-curricular activities and knowledge, and professionals who are after inspiration and a sensorial experience to enrich their daily work.
The event attracted the attention of students, designers, artists, illustrators, advertisers and enthusiasts.
